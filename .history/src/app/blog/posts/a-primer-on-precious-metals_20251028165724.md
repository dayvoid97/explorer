## Few items seemilgly important but irrelevant

There are some questions we like to make big deal out of, however, are insignificant when factored in. Questions that begin with the 'China' word, like how much gold does China hold, how fast is China's holdings increasing, how big is the Chinese gold market, ignore these questions. Gold, in its global definition, is a precious commodity coveted globally that _also_ has industrial application and is generally considered to be a safe-haven asset by people worldwide. Major central banks across the world hold significant amount of gold, and the metal has always had a history of being coveted by regular folks alike. Some cultures go nuts for gold, others haven't had reasons to be so. Either way, gold's place as a below-risk asset holds true globally.

Important to consider is the fact that central banks have more than doubled their gold holdings while decreasing treasury and other paper holdings for their own reasons. Gold as a part of culture is going to stay irregardless of its price in regions across the globe where it already has strong hold. Insititutions, private investors and ETFs will most certainly increase their physical holdings as it becomes increasingly more attractive to hold and offer gold-based financial products.
